THE WAR

Why do we have to fight so that there be light?
The hills in their heights have been where in frights at night,
We out of sight run to escape our plight,
We still feel cold even though we hold ourselves tight,
Oh! We say, “death’s knight is risen against the peaceful elites.”

Why does every ground around us in blood pound?
No safe place have we found?
Or no one can hear our cry as it sounds?

Shall the sun glow so that we’d have hopes for tomorrow?
Cause even our bone marrow gets harrowed at our sorrow
And we are confused that our instincts we don’t follow.

Why, like hay we are cast away?
Or we try to stay, they slay,
And this is not our home they say every day.

I only wish I could go back to the past and change this life’s cast
So that peace we could have at last.
This, if I had the chance I would do fast.

I lift up my eyes to the sky and I cry
But there was no one to hear the drop of the tears of my fears
as it drops fierce and I sigh.
Does anyone care to take me up the stairs of my scares?

If a word can change the world,
Then why a sword to us ward is put forward,
And war not peace on us is bestowed.

Caskets like baskets are sold in the market,
We are warmed so at cold we don’t need jackets,
Hatred and misery to us are given in packets
it’s for free so we don’t need tickets
We are been thrown and shot like a ball in crickets

Why do we all have to scatter?
The soil filled with decaying matter,
You offer me war or peace, I’ll choose the latter
That out of this weight, I’ll feel lighter.

On the plain we are slain,
Bullets on us falling like rain,
Our clothes, full of blood stain,
We had wished we die because we can’t bear the pain.

Why does hatred like fire rise higher,
Every man in his own path a liar,
I’ve thought so much about it that now I’m tired,
If any being other than God can stop this I would have hired,
But I know they’ve all tried and have retired.

See the tragedy we have obtained,
The heights of war we have attained,
Wickedness has our hearts contained,
Harsh memories have we retained,
That the peace of ourselves we no longer maintain,
The end of the war we can’t ascertain.
Oh! Death, why are you restrained?

If death was health,
Then more will I buy with my last breath,
And there would have been no need for health,
Cause we all would have been rich from birth.

This war I hate,
And in faith I wait for the date of my fate,
Oh! Death, don’t be late.

These questions I ask out of tension,
They are much but a few I mention,
It is out of passion to ward off depression,

And let this test be a quest,
So that of the rest I’ll be the first and best.

Let this time be the end of the line that our minds be refined,
That peace may sublime and I’ll rest from these troubles of mine,
At least I should say that we are all fine,
Cause peace has become our spine.

Now, if my death be so inevitable,
Then let through my fable I am able to strengthen the feeble
and bring together the huddle that when I go,
my name in their hearts becomes indelible and for that I am humbled.

By Wilson Zimthamaha
